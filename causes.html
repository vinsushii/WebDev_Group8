Causes of Racism

Historical

Foundations

Historical Foundations

Racism has deep roots in historical events such as colonization, slavery, and segregation. These events created lasting stereotypes and unequal systems of power.

  • Colonial exploitation and oppression
  • Slavery and forced labor
  • Segregation and apartheid laws
  • Generational trauma and inherited prejudice

Psychological

Factors

Psychological Factors

Racism is partly driven by human psychology — the way people categorize others into groups and react to differences.

  • In-group vs. out-group bias
  • Fear of the unfamiliar (xenophobia)
  • Stereotyping and scapegoating
  • Social learning from family or peers

Systemic

Reinforcement

Systemic Reinforcement

Racism is reinforced by social, economic, and political systems that favor certain groups over others.

  • Discriminatory laws and policies
  • Inequities in education, jobs, and housing
  • Biased criminal justice systems
  • Unequal access to healthcare and resources
