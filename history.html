History

This is the History Page of the Website

This section delves into the historical context of racism, tracing its origins and evolution over time.

Timeline: History of Racism

1600s–1700s

Race emerges as a social and political category rooted in European colonialism and the transatlantic slave trade.

Late 1800s – Early 1900s

Jim Crow laws in the United States institutionalize segregation and disenfranchisement of Black Americans.

1921

Tulsa Race Massacre: A prosperous Black community was destroyed by white mobs in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

1964-1965

The Civil Rights Act and Voting Rights Act are passed in the U.S., major steps in legally ending segregation.

Present Day

Recognition of systemic racism: ongoing disparities in education, health, and housing drive global racial justice movements.
