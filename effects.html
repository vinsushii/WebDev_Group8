EFFECTS OF RACISM

Physical Effects

There have been many researches regarding the connection between racism and physical health to suggest that the stress that comes with experiencing racism can have long-lasting effects. It can heighten blood pressure which weakens the immune system, raising the risk of developing long-term health conditions. Racism has been linked with higher stress rates, increasing a minority's risk of developing high blood pressure.

Mental Effects

In an article that reviews multiple other articles that discuss the effects of racism to the mental health, it mentioned that the most frequently reported mental health outcome was depression which was reported in 37.2% of the articles. Other mental health outcomes included the self-esteem which was reported in 24.3% of them, psychological stress reported in 21.3%, distress reported in 18.3%, and lastly anxiety reported in 14.4% of the articles.

Hate crimes against Asian American people and Pacific Islanders surged during the COVID-19 pandemic, and a 2021 study found that COVID-19-related racial/ethnic discrimination had links with a greater risk of depression, anxiety, self-harm, binge drinking, and suicidal ideation among Asian American and Pacific Islander students.
